About Lumina

What is this thing?

So Lumina is basically a search engine interface i made using just plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No fancy frameworks or anything, just the good old web stuff. It's like a privacy-focused alternative to those big search pages that are super heavy and track everything you do.

Why'd I make it?

I got tired of search engines that feel slow and cluttered, so I built this thing that puts privacy first and lets you customize everything. It's got weather, notes, bookmarks, and a bunch of other stuff all in one place. Plus it looks pretty nice with all the Material Design stuff.

Cool features it has

Search Stuff:
Works with DuckDuckGo, Google, Bing, and even your own custom search engine. Plus voice search if your browser supports it!
Personalization:
12 different color themes, custom backgrounds, and you can drag widgets around wherever you want.
Productivity:
Notes with markdown, bookmarks, weather, clock, calendar, and quick action buttons.
Privacy:
Doesn't track you, stores everything locally, and uses privacy-focused APIs.

How it works under the hood

Tech Stack: Just some blessed HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. No npm packages or build bloat needed!

APIs: Gets weather from Open-Meteo (which is privacy-focused) and uses IP location for weather.

Storage: Everything saves to your browser's local storage, and you can export/import your settings.

About me (the creator)

I'm just some developer who likes making web apps that are fast and respect your privacy. I believe in keeping things simple but powerful - no unnecessary complexity. This project is open source so anyone can contribute or learn from it!

Privacy stuff

  • Your searches don't get stored or sent anywhere
  • All your settings stay in your browser
  • Weather location is just approximate (from your IP)
  • No tracking scripts or analytics junk
  • Totally transparent about what data it uses

What's next?

I'll keep making it faster and adding features that actually help people be more productive. Accessibility improvements and maybe more customization options are on the list too. All while keeping the privacy-first approach!

Wanna help or have ideas?

Your feedback makes this better! If you're a developer, feel free to contribute. If you're a user with suggestions, I'd love to hear them. Let's make the web a bit more private and user-friendly together.
